UDPL Man-On-Man Match Information –

This competition will have 2 shooters that start facing matching arrays with a crossing stop plate. At the start signal, each shooter will shoot their complete array and then their stop plate. The shooter whose stop plate is on the bottom will be the winner of the bout. If either shooter shoots the stop plate and they still have a plate/popper left standing, they will be declared the looser of that bout. In the event both shooters prematurely shoot the stop plate, the shooter with the most of their array complete will be declared the winner, if both arrays match, the stop plate on the bottom will be declared the winner.

Standard J-ladder pairings will be used to run each squad. At the beginning of each stage, there will be a random drawing to fill the first column of the ladder. Each pair of shooters will shoot the best two out of three. For each bout the shooter wins, they will be awarded one point. The winner will advance to the next round on the right side of the ladder and the looser will move to the left side of the ladder for the second elimination. The winner of the left side of the bracket will move over to face the winner of the right side of the ladder.

There will be a winner for each class/squad. The shooter with the most points after all stages are complete will be declared the winner. In the event of a tie, it will be decided by who won when the tied shooters met throughout the day. In the event the tie continues, the shooter that advanced the farthest in any ladder will be the tie breaker. If the tie continues, they will shoot off the best two out of three.

There will be two divisions for the UDPL Man-On-Man event:

  • HiCap: No optical sights or compensators.
  • Open: Anything goes within the bounds of safety.


There will be a handicap system to match the shooters when HiCap meets Open at the line, which will be one plate put down on the HiCap shooter’s array before the bout.

There will be three classes for these matches:

  • Top shot - Heads up class - no handicapping (class or division) anyone welcome in this class, but IDPA Master or Expert and USPSA/ICORE "A" must compete in this class.
  • Straight shot - IDPA Marksman or Sharp Shooter and USPSA/ICORE "B" or "C" class fall into this category. In this class, there will be a 1-second handicap per division of separation. If HiCap and Open match up, 1 plate will be put down on the HiCap side.
  • Have shot - All other shooters, IDPA Novice or Unclassified and USPSA/ICORE "D" class. In this class, there will be a 1 second handicap per division of separation. If Hicap and Open match up, 1 plate will be put down on the HiCap side.

 

Additional Information and clarification 2/05/13 –
Many of you have questions about this match for which I will try to provide some answers. If there are some questions I don’t address below, please post them on the UDPL Facebook page and I will check it regularly.

The basic premise of this match is that two shooters will start facing matching array’s and the first to finish their array and their stop plate wins the bout. At each match up, the winner of the best 2 out of 3 bouts will advance. When a shooter loses their first match up, they will move into the second-chance bracket to face another shooter. After a second loss, the shooter is eliminated on that stage. The three separate stages stand on their own like three separate matches. For every bout a shooter wins, they will receive 1 point. We have solved the scoring problems from the beta match last July and points will be earned on BOTH sides of the J-ladder. Each of the three classes stand on their own. The person with the most points on the stage will win the stage, and the person with the most overall points for all three stages will win their class. You can find an example of the action that can be expected here. http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0USAH-Pvjas

First question: Will I drive clear out there only to shoot once or twice and go home.
The answer to that is NO. There will be three separate stages with a double elimination at each stage. Even if someone shot abnormally badly and lost every bout, they would still meet up with 6 shooters best 2 of 3 which would be at least 12 bouts. With the handicap system in place, the likelihood of losing every bout is very slim even for shooters that have just begun shooting.

Second question: At the beta match we held last summer, I didn’t earn points in the second chance bracket - has that changed?
The points system has been changed and points will be earned on both sides of the J-ladder. If you win any bout you will earn the point for it. (There is a 2-point add-on at the second bracket on the right to balance the points system).

Third question: How many rounds do I need?
This match does not have a fixed round count because it depends on how far thru the J-ladder a shooter progresses. This can be a high round count match in excess of 300 rounds. Best estimate would be the overall winner of the stage will shoot 120 rounds on the stage and there are three stages.

Forth question: Will there be a shoot-off to determine an overall winner of the match?
At this time there is not a plan to have a shoot-off because of time constraints. There will be a first, second, third, fourth, and so on in each class.

Fifth question: How will I be squaded; will I shoot with my friend?
There will be three squads for each class. The squad you are in will be determined by your ability level. (Myself, I find anyone I shoot with to be my friend).
